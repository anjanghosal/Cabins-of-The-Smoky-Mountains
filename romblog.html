Romantic Getaways

Tyler Schwaeble Jun 14 • 3 min read

Ole Smoky Candy Kitchen: Need a cure for your sweet tooth? Look no further than the Ole Smoky Candy Kitchen. The intricate process of handmaking taffy is displayed in the front of the store and samples are given out to spectators. There are a bunch of treats available for purchase, spanning from chocolate, caramel apples, fudge, and peanut brittle. Take a look at their website for the full menu and locations.

Gatlinburg Trolley: Travel throughout Gatlinburg on a trolley! There are over 100 stops for these trolleys, all of which are color coded according to their routes. The routes are as follows: Downtown River Rd., Red Route; Downtown Parkway, Green Route; Arts & Crafts Community, Yellow Route; National Park, Tan Route; Dollywood, Pink Route; East Parkway and City Hall, Blue Route; North Gatlinburg Parkway and Welcome Center, Purple Route. The 20 active trolleys service a 50 mile-area with fares range from 50 cents to $2. More information regarding the Gatlinburg Trolleys can be found here on their website.


Gatlinburg Farmer's Market: The Gatlinburg Farmer’s Market is in its seventh season and offers local Tennessee-grown produce, food and products. There are a variety of different seasonal products offered at the market like fruits, nuts, berries, honey, jellies and jams. There are also baked treats, plants and other wellness products available for purchase. Every second Saturday of the month, the market holds events with music and activities for children. The Farmer’s Market captured the essence of Gatlinburg and is a relaxing time for those looking for a calmer environment.

Gatlinburg Mysterious Mansion: But if you’re looking for excitement there’s no better place to find it than the Gatlinburg Mystery Mansion. The house was designed and decorated in an early 1900’s style old Victorian persona and has been around since 1980 making the Gatlinburg Mysterious Mansion the oldest haunted house in Gatlinburg. The house is open for business from Friday through Sunday with varying hours. Check out all of the thrills and terror this haunted house has to offer here on their website.


Alewine Pottery: This family owned, and operated business has grown to be one of the most iconic businesses in the Smokies. Alewine Pottery is now nationally renowned as the company has grown since it was established over 30 years ago. The beautiful ceramic craftsmanship and the friendly, fun-loving atmosphere makes Alewine Pottery a must-see destination for those visiting Gatlinburg or Pigeon Forge. If you’re looking to browse the pottery collection you can do so on their website here.

The Village Shops in Gatlinburg: The Village Shops is a collection of 27 different unique shops and eateries. All 27 stores are European-themed to replicate an Old-World atmosphere. There are places to shop for clothing, artwork, and even Smoky Mountain mementos. No trip to Gatlinburg would be complete without visiting the Village, for more information on shops and locations browse their website here.


Scenic Helicopter Tour: There’s nothing quite like a bird’s eye view of the Great Smoky Mountains… except for the view from a helicopter. Elevating well over hundreds of feet in the air, the Scenic Helicopter Tour is sure to awe as it gives you a new perspective of the world around you. Starting at just $29 per person, Scenic Helicopter Tours is ideal for anyone looking for intimate and unique views of the Smokies. More information about the different tours can be found on their website here.

Biltmore Estate: Located a little over 80 miles east of Gatlinburg, this 8,000-acre estate includes the 250-room Biltmore House, an award-winning winery, and 75 acres of landscaped gardens. The Biltmore House is the main attraction, coming in at a whopping 175,000 square-feet! There are several tours of the property spanning from the winery, to the spa, the gardens and landscaping, and the Biltmore House itself. There are 15 different restaurants that serve fresh produce, meat and wine that was made on the estate. The diverse list of activities offered includes several wine tastings, carriage rides, shooting lessons, fishing trips, hiking, yoga classes, bike rentals, horseback riding, falconry, rafting and much more! The complete list of activities can be found on their website here.
