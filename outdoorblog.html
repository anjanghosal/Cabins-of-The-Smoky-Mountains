Outdoor Adventures

Tyler Schwaeble Jun 14 • 3 min read

The Great Smoky Mountains: A trip to Gatlinburg wouldn’t be complete without visiting the trails of the Great Smoky Mountain National Park. The park is ideal for anyone looking for a trek through peaceful walkways, birdwatching, horseback rides, biking, or just some quality time with nature. The Smokies are home to over 150 trails all varying in difficulty, ensuring that anyone from a novice to the most experienced hiker will enjoy. Is hiking not your cup of tea? That’s ok, as you can still experience nature without any of the inconveniences with Auto Touring. Stay in the comfort of your car and experience scenic views, mountain streams, waterfalls, beautiful wildflowers, historic buildings and mature hardwood forests. See a complete list of all that the Great Smoky Mountains has to offer website.

Anakeesta: This mountain theme park is famous for their beautiful views. Explore the Smokies, Downtown Gatlinburg, and the Vista Gardens all at Anakeesta. Admission includes your choice of either a Chondola or Ridge Rambler adventure truck ride to the entrance of Firefly Village located in the summit of Anakeesta Mountain. In addition to their views, Anakeesta is home to the country’s only single-rail mountain coaster. The exhilarating fun doesn’t stop there either with their Dueling Zipline Adventure. The three side-by-side ziplines are suspended over 150 feet and race through the mountain with some fun challenge stations on the way. More information can be found on their website.


Ober Gatlinburg: Ober Gatlinburg isn’t just your premier skiing destination. Ober Gatlinburg offers several other fun outdoor activities like snowboarding, tubing, ice skating, chairlifts… and so much more! This mountain amusement park is packed with attractions like the Aerial Tramway, Ski Mountain Coaster, Alpine Slides, and a Wildlife Encounter! Ober Gatlinburg is their own four-seasons world of fun, more details can be found on their website.

Smoky Mountain Outdoors White-Water Raftin: Looking for unique views of the Smokies? Try white water rafting at the Smoky Mountain Outdoors. Rush down five and a half miles of beautiful mountain scenery while gaining a meaningful appreciation of The Great Smoky Mountains. This is a perfect activity for large groups of 10 or more, more information regarding group discounts and booking can be found on their website here.


Smoky Mountain Horseback Riding Stables: Peacefully view the Smoky Mountains atop a horse at Smoky Mountain Horseback Riding Stables. No prior experience is necessary, and those older than five can experience these walking speed trail rides. With all of their well-trained horses, Smoky Mountain Horseback Riding Stables guarantees an amazing time for both the most novice and experienced riders. More information regarding safety and pricing can be found on their website here.
